Proof of 4.56: Only distributivity and commutativity need to be checked. One dis-
tributivity law is easy: Given f , g : Si+j+k→Sj+k and h : Sj+k→Sk , then h(f + g) =
hf + hg since both expressions equal hf and hg on the two hemispheres of Si+j+k .
The other distributivity law will follow from this one and the commutativity relation.
To prove the commutativity relation it will be convenient to express suspension
in terms of smash product. The smash product Sn ∧S1 can be regarded as the quo-
tient space of Sn×I with Sn×∂I ∪{x0}×I collapsed to a point. This is the same
as the quotient of the suspension Sn+1 of Sn obtained by collapsing to a point the
suspension of x0 . Collapsing this arc in Sn+1 to a point again yields Sn+1 , so we
obtain in this way a homeomorphism identifying Sn ∧S1 with Sn+1 . Under this iden-
tiﬁcation the suspension Sf of a basepoint-preserving map f : Sn→Sn becomes the
smash product f ∧11 : Sn ∧S1→Sn ∧S1 . By iteration, the k fold suspension Skf then
corresponds to f ∧11 : Sn ∧Sk→Sn ∧Sk .
Now we verify the commutativity relation.
