6 The complex logarithm
Suppose we wish to deﬁne the logarithm of a non-zero complex num-
ber.
If z = reiθ, and we want the logarithm to be the inverse to the
exponential, then it is natural to set
log z = log r + iθ.
Here and below, we use the convention that log r denotes the standard 1
logarithm of the positive number r. The trouble with the above deﬁni-
tion is that θ is unique only up to an integer multiple of 2π. However,

or sheet of the logarithm.
