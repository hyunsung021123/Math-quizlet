Suppose we are given a pair of functions f and F analytic in regions
Ωand Ω′, respectively, with Ω⊂Ω′. If the two functions agree on the
smaller set Ω, we say that F is an analytic continuation of f into the
region Ω′. The corollary then guarantees that there can be only one such
analytic continuation, since F is uniquely determined by f.

This is a striking result that is obviously not true in the case of real

not be diﬀerentiable. For example, we know that every continuous func-

tion is diﬀerentiable.
