53
Suppose we are given a pair of functions f and F analytic in regions
Ωand Ω′, respectively, with Ω⊂Ω′. If the two functions agree on the
smaller set Ω, we say that F is an analytic continuation of f into the
region Ω′. The corollary then guarantees that there can be only one such
analytic continuation, since F is uniquely determined by f.

5.1 Morera’s theorem
A direct application of what was proved here is a converse of Cauchy’s
theorem.
Theorem 5.1 Suppose f is a continuous function in the open disc D
such that for any triangle T contained in D

Proof.
By the proof of Theorem 2.1 the function f has a primitive F
in D that satisﬁes F ′ = f. By the regularity theorem, we know that F
is indeﬁnitely (and hence twice) complex diﬀerentiable, and therefore f
is holomorphic.
