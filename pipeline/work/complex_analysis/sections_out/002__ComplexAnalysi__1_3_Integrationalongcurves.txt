It is also clear how to deﬁne a piecewise-smooth curve. The points
z(a) and z(b) are called the end-points of the curve and are independent
on the parametrization. Since γ carries an orientation, it is natural to
say that γ begins at z(a) and ends at z(b).
A smooth or piecewise-smooth curve is closed if z(a) = z(b) for any
of its parametrizations. Finally, a smooth or piecewise-smooth curve is
simple if it is not self-intersecting, that is, z(t)̸ = z(s) unless s = t. Of
course, if the curve is closed to begin with, then we say that it is simple
whenever z(t)̸ = z(s) unless s = t, or s = a and t = b.
