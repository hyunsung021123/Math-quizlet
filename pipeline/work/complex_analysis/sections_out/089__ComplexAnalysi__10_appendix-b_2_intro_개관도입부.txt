The idea of the proof of Theorem 2.1 can be roughly summarized as
follows. Since the complement of Γ is open, it is suﬃcient to show it
is pathwise connected (Exercise 5, Chapter 1). Let z and w belong to
the complement of Γ, and join these two points by a curve. If this curve
intersects Γ, we ﬁrst connect z to z′ and w to w′, where z′ and w′ are
close to Γ, by curves that do not intersect Γ. Then, we join z′ to w′
by traveling “parallel” to the curve Γ and going around its end-points if
necessary.

cannot happen. Consequently, O1 is open, and by the same argument, so
is O2. Finally, we claim that O1 is non-empty. If not, then F1 is entirely
contained in Γ and U is contained in F2. Pick any point z ∈F1, which
we know belongs to Γ. Now every ball centered at z intersects U, hence

similar argument for O1 proves that
U = O1 ∪O2,

fact that U is connected, and concludes the proof of the Jordan curve
